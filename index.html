Research

Zn Electrodeposition for Metal Batteries · University of Michigan

I study zinc electrodeposition in ionic liquid and other non-aqueous electrolytes using high-throughput microelectrode arrays. By operating under controlled current conditions and imaging the resulting morphologies (mossy, compact, dendritic) with optical microscopy, SEM, EDS, and XPS, I link deposition pathways to Coulombic efficiency and reversibility. I use extracted diffusion coefficients and exchange current densities to build COMSOL models that guide current-density windows for stable Zn cycling and improved Zn anode design.

Thermoelectric Materials and Band Structure Modeling · IIT Bombay

Previously, I worked on n-type Mg3Sb0.6Bi1.4 and related solid solutions for waste-heat thermoelectrics. I combined synthesis, transport measurements (conductivity, Seebeck, Hall), and multiband electronic band-structure modeling in MATLAB to generate 3D predictive maps of doping, temperature, and zT. This helped identify broad high-zT regions, improving temperature-averaged efficiency and widening the processing and stability window.

Magnesium Alloys for Lightweight Automotive Applications · ARAI, Pune

I also evaluated AZ91D magnesium alloys as lightweight replacements for aluminum components in automobiles. By correlating microstructure and mechanical properties and assessing creep and corrosion behavior, I estimated that a 10% vehicle weight reduction can yield roughly 5% fuel savings.
