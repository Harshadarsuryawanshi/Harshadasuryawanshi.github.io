Research Experience

Electrodeposition Study on Zn Metal Anodes
Aug 2023 – Present  |  University of Michigan
Graduate Student Researcher  •  Advisor: Prof. Yiyang Li
  • Investigating zinc electrodeposition morphology in ionic liquid and other non-aqueous electrolytes using high-throughput microelectrode arrays.
  • Analyzed current-controlled electrodeposition to differentiate dendritic and mossy morphologies and their impact on Coulombic efficiency using optical microscopy, SEM, and EDS.
  • Extracted Zn2+ diffusion coefficients and exchange current density to parameterize a COMSOL model of the electrochemical system.
  • Predicted optimal current densities for zinc metal anodes, informing safer and more efficient zinc-based energy storage system design.
Thermoelectric Materials and Devices
Nov 2021 – Jun 2023  |  IIT Bombay
Graduate Student Researcher  •  Advisor: Prof. Titas Dasgupta
  • Synthesized n-type Mg3Sb2–Mg3Bi2 solid solutions (0–3% Te) targeting zT values exceeding commercial Bi2Te3 for sustainable power generation.
  • Measured temperature-dependent electrical conductivity, Seebeck coefficient, and Hall coefficient to characterize charge transport.
  • Developed MATLAB tools to extract band mass and carrier mobility via a single parabolic band model.
  • Implemented a multiband least-squares fitting framework to reconstruct the electronic band structure and identify promising compositional windows for high zT.
Magnesium Alloys for Automotive Applications
Jun 2021 – Aug 2021  |  ARAI, Pune
Summer Research Intern  •  Advisor: Prasanna Deshmukh
  • Evaluated AZ91D magnesium alloys as lightweight replacements for aluminum in automotive components under fuel-efficiency constraints.
  • Correlated microstructure and mechanical properties across compositions and compiled a design-focused report.
  • Assessed creep and corrosion behavior to guide alloy selection; projected ~5% fuel-use reduction for a 10% vehicle weight decrease.
